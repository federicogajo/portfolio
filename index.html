MEET Museum

Exhibit

Digital Forest is an installation that shows the digital encounter between animals and the human context, and traces the transformation from wild entities captured by trail cams to digital entities categorized by users on social media. The project investigates how animals, inadvertently turned into digital entities, inhabit the online ecosystem, a space categorized by humans for human consumption.

2023

Speculative design

Natural disasters, climate anomalies, humanitarian crises. We are surrounded by screens full of images about climate change that provoke eco-anxiety. Litia, a tech company, presents a protection to all of this: Specta, your personal eco-anxiety silencer. Studies and research based on the rock’s millennial knowledge, prove that climate change has always been there and always will be; that our biocentric view is limited; and that eco-anxiety is a meaningless feeling. Through Specta, it is possible to watch the same events related to climate change from the perspective of the rocks, seen as calming stratifications, and enter into a state of contemplation.

Team members:

Francesco Bonetti

Emiliano Garibaldi

Enrico Isidori

Cecilia Pizzagalli

2024

Editorial design

INATTO is a magazine written by creatives for creatives with the aim of opening a discussion on the needs of those who belong to the field. The magazine header works as a constitution while each issue of it is a new right, which is legitimised through the words of the people who have participated in its creation. The magazine presents itself as a place for dialogue between different points of view, with the aim of questioning those taboos that prevent the creative system from evolving. The right to copy is the first issue.

Team members:

Miguel Amaya

Ginevra Bernasconi

Giulia Bonalumi

2024

Editorial design

"Anthropology of Intelligences: On Risk and Opportunities of AI in the Study of Culture” is a book presenting the class material from the “AI and Anthropology” course of the Master in Artificial Intelligence. Across six chapters, the book outlines the various ways in which AI can influence anthropology, providing a comprehensive overview of its potentials and risks. Above all, the lessons summarized in the book explore the biases in AI, explaining how these reflect and influence our representation of the world and its facets. Both the trailer video and all the textual content of the book were entirely written by artificial intelligence itself. Within it, the promptography contain all the questions we asked to Chat GPT in order to product the content of the book.

Team members:

Ginevra Bernasconi

Nicola Maria Patitucci

Francesco Scarfone

Carlotta Sereni

Michele Venini

2024

Web Design

What does your discomfort look like? Unplaced is a web design project that collects faint imaginary places, disarming in their concreteness, emerging directly from the most veiled unconscious of our minds. In psychology, emotional fields can be interpreted as predominantly imaginary places of thought that live and feed in a primarily unconscious dimension. What form does this place take? How do you imagine it?

Emiliano Garibaldi

Nicola Maria Patitucci

Giga Design studio

6:AM

Editorial design

Portfolio designed for 6:AM, a Murano glassworks that stands out for its approach to glassmaking, based on a strong synergy between empirical artistry and a solid knowledge of ancient techniques. The layout, originally conceived as a downloadable PDF from the website, was designed to ensure great flexibility of use. The margins and single-page format were thoughtfully designed to provide optimal reproduction even from an office printer, allowing the company to print only the desired and necessary pages for the client, at any time and in any situation.

Team members:

Enrico Isidori

Diego Massaro
